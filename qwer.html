ABOUT AMRITSAR

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple is also called the “Darbar Sahib” or the “Harmandar Sahib”. Located in Amritsar, this is one of the oldest worship places for Indian Sikhs.

Jaliwala Bagh

The memorial at this site commemorates the 2000 Indians who were killed or wounded, shot indiscriminately by the British under the command of Gen Michael O’Dyer on April 13, 1919, during a peaceful public meeting. This was a major incident in India's freedom struggle.

Wagah Border

Wagah Border is located 32 km from Amritsar, Punjab. It lies on the Grand Trunk Road connecting Amritsar (India) and Lahore (Pakistan). The daily flag-lowering ceremony at this border is a major attraction for tourists.

Amritsari Kulcha

Amritsar is known as the food capital of Punjab. One of the most famous street foods is Amritsari Kulcha, a stuffed flatbread enjoyed hot with butter. It is a humble take on the famous aloo ka paratha.
